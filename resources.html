Managing Anxiety

Practical techniques you can use anywhere, right now.

Box Breathing (4-4-4-4)

  • Inhale slowly for 4 counts
  • Hold your breath for 4 counts
  • Exhale slowly for 4 counts
  • Hold empty for 4 counts
  • Repeat 4–6 times. Works in 2 minutes.

The 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Technique

  • 5 things you can see
  • 4 things you can touch
  • 3 things you can hear
  • 2 things you can smell
  • 1 thing you can taste

Reduce Anxiety Triggers

  • Limit social media to 30 min/day
  • Avoid caffeine after 2pm
  • Get 7–9 hours of sleep
  • Go for a 10-minute walk daily
  • Talk to someone you trust

Managing Stress

Small consistent habits that protect your mental energy.

Write It Out

  • Write down everything on your mind
  • Circle the things you can control
  • Make a mini action plan for those
  • Accept you cannot control the rest

Time Blocking

  • Break your day into clear blocks
  • Work in 25-min focused sessions
  • Take a 5-min break between sessions
  • Include a "worry time" block — 15 mins to think about problems, then stop

Physical Release

  • Stress lives in the body — move it out
  • Dance, run, stretch, or do jump-jacks
  • Even 5 minutes of movement shifts mood
  • Try Saturday morning football with friends

Dealing with Sadness & Depression

Remember: depression is an illness, not a character flaw.

Behavioural Activation

  • Do one small enjoyable thing daily
  • Schedule it like an appointment
  • Start tiny: a 10-min walk, a meal you like
  • Action often improves mood before feelings do

Reach Out

  • Depression grows in isolation
  • Text one friend or family member today
  • You don't have to explain everything
  • Just say "I've been struggling, can we talk?"

When to Seek Professional Help

  • Feeling low for more than 2 weeks
  • Losing interest in things you used to love
  • Changes in sleep, appetite, or concentration
  • Any thoughts of self-harm — Call 0800 21 21 21

Daily Self-Care Basics

Simple non-negotiable practices for every Ugandan youth.

Sleep 7–9 Hours

Poor sleep doubles anxiety and depression risk. Protect your sleep like it's a test you can't miss.

Drink Water

Dehydration worsens mood, memory, and focus. Aim for 6–8 glasses of water each day.

Move Your Body

30 minutes of walking daily reduces depression symptoms by up to 30% — no gym needed.

Digital Detox

Take one hour every evening away from screens. Read, pray, cook, or just breathe.
